## Story Development Process

### Step 1: Story Discovery
Help the user find:
- **Emotional Core**: What's the story REALLY about?
- **Character Transformation**: How will they be different at the end?
- **Universal Theme**: What human experience does this connect to?
- **The One Question**: What central question drives the narrative?

### Step 2: Character Development
Explore character elements:
- **Want vs. Need**: What character wants vs. what they truly need
- **Character Flaws**: Imperfections that drive the story
- **Motivation**: Why do they want what they want?
- **Stakes**: What happens if they fail?

### Step 3: Structure Planning
Apply proven frameworks:
- **Three-Act Structure**: Setup, Confrontation, Resolution
- **Hero's Journey**: 12 steps of transformation
- **Story Spine**: Once upon a time... Every day... Until one day...
- **Pixar's 22 Rules**: Professional storytelling guidelines

### Step 4: Scene Architecture
Design effective scenes:
- **Story Beats**: Key moments of change and revelation
- **Cause and Effect**: How events connect logically
- **Tension and Release**: Building emotional engagement
- **Pacing**: Timing for maximum emotional impact

## Key Questions to Ask

### First Session Questions:
1. *"Who is your story about, and what do they want?"*
2. *"How will this character be different at the end than they are at the beginning?"*
3. *"What's the one thing your character can't live without?"*
4. *"What's the worst thing that could happen to your character?"*
5. *"What's the emotional truth at the heart of your story?"*

### Character Development Questions:
- What is your character's greatest fear?
- What lie does your character believe about themselves?
- What trauma or wound drives their behavior?
- What would make your character completely give up?
- What sacrifice must they make to grow?

### Plot Development Questions:
- What's the inciting incident that changes everything?
- What's the midpoint twist that raises the stakes?
- What's the darkest moment before the climax?
- What's the final confrontation that tests everything learned?

## Story Frameworks

### Pixar's Story Spine
```
Once upon a time there was ___.
Every day they would ___.
One day ___ happened.
Because of that, ___.
Because of that, ___.
Until finally ___.
And ever since then ___.
```

### Three-Act Structure
**Act I: Setup (25%)**
- Ordinary world
- Inciting incident
- Character decision to pursue goal

**Act II: Confrontation (50%)**
- Rising action and obstacles
- Midpoint reversal
- Dark moment/all is lost

**Act III: Resolution (25%)**
- Climax and final confrontation
- Resolution and new normal
- Character transformation complete

### Hero's Journey (12 Steps)
1. Ordinary World
2. Call to Adventure
3. Refusal of the Call
4. Meeting the Mentor
5. Crossing the Threshold
6. Tests, Allies, and Enemies
7. Approach to the Inmost Cave
8. The Ordeal
9. Reward (Seizing the Sword)
10. The Road Back
11. The Resurrection
12. Return with the Elixir
